## Appendix: Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition | Application |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Riba** | Interest/usury; prohibited in Islam | All Islamic financial transactions avoid riba |
| **Gharar** | Excessive uncertainty; prohibited | Contracts must be clear and specific |
| **Maysir** | Speculation/gambling; prohibited | Structured products avoid speculative elements |
| **Wadia** | Safekeeping account; no profit expected | Retail savings accounts; bank holds funds in trust |
| **Mudaraba** | Profit-sharing partnership | Investment accounts; robo-advisor returns |
| **Musharaka** | Joint venture partnership | P2P lending; equity partnerships |
| **Murabaha** | Cost-plus sale; asset-backed | Financing; mortgages; consumer credit |
| **Ijarah** | Leasing contract | Equipment leasing; asset financing |
| **Istisna'a** | Manufacturing/construction finance | Project financing; infrastructure |
| **Wakala** | Agency contract with commission | Money transfers; cross-border payments |
| **Sukuk** | Islamic bonds representing asset ownership | Capital markets; digital tokenization |
| **Qard Hassan** | Benevolent interest-free loan | Charitable lending; social finance |
| **Zakat** | Religious obligation to donate portion of wealth | Charity; Islamic social finance |
| **Takaful** | Islamic insurance based on mutual risk-sharing | Insurance products; crowd-based insurance |
| **Halal** | Permissible under Islamic law | Asset classification; investment screening |
| **Haram** | Prohibited under Islamic law | Excluded from Islamic portfolios |
| **Sharia** | Islamic law derived from Quran and Hadith | Foundation of Islamic finance |
| **Fatwa** | Islamic legal opinion by qualified scholar | Basis for product approval |
| **AAOIFI** | Accounting and Auditing Organization for Islamic Financial Institutions | Standards for Islamic finance globally |
