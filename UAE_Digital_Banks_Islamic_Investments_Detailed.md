# UAE Digital Banks: Islamic Compliance & Investment Products Deep Dive

## Islamic Banking Compliance Analysis

### 1. ADIB - Abu Dhabi Islamic Bank (100% Sharia-Compliant)

#### Compliance Framework
- **Status**: Only 100% Islamic bank among comparison group
- **Regulator**: Central Bank of UAE
- **Sharia Governance**: Dedicated Sharia unit + Fatwa Board
- **Audit**: Internal Sharia Audit function
- **Staff**: Trained and certified in Islamic banking

#### Sharia Compliance Details
**All products use Islamic financing structures:**
- **Murabaha** (Profit-based financing): Cost-plus profit model
- **Ijara** (Leasing): Asset-based leasing structures
- **Mudarabah** (Partnership): Investment partnerships with profit-sharing
- **Sukuk** (Islamic bonds): Asset-backed investment certificates
- **Takaful** (Islamic insurance): Risk-sharing insurance model
- **Qard Hasan** (Benevolent loan): Interest-free lending for current accounts

#### Product Categories
**Deposit Products:**
- Current Account (Qard Hasan structure)
- Savings Account (Mudarabah-based)
- Investment Deposit (Wakalah structure)
- Prize-linked Savings (Ghina Account)

**Financing Products:**
- Personal Finance (Murabaha)
- Auto Finance (Ijara or Murabaha)
- Home Finance (Ijara)

**Investment Products:**
- Sukuk Platform (starting AED 1,000 USD)
- Securities (separate platform)
- Insurance through Takaful

**Cards:**
- All Covered Cards (Visa/Mastercard) are Sharia-compliant
- No Riba (interest) charging
- SHARE program based on Rebate structure

#### Certification
- Each product has individual Sharia Fatwa approval
- Board of independent Islamic scholars
- Regular Sharia Audit reports

#### Pros
✓ Complete Islamic banking guarantee
✓ 100% compliance for Practicing Muslims
✓ No conventional/Islamic confusion
✓ Strong Sharia governance
✓ Wide product range (all Islamic)

#### Cons
✗ No conventional banking option
✗ Interest calculations different (profit-based)
✗ Limited investment in equities
✗ Smaller investment product portfolio vs peers

### 2. FAB Islamic (First Abu Dhabi Bank - Dual Structure)

#### Compliance Framework
- **Status**: Dual system - Conventional + Islamic division
- **Islamic Division**: FAB Islamic (separate books of accounts)
- **Regulator**: Central Bank of UAE
- **Sharia Governance**: Sharia Fatwa and Supervisory Board
- **Scope**: All business lines have Islamic equivalents

#### Sharia Compliance Details
**Islamic Structures:**
- **Qard Hasan**: Interest-free current accounts
- **Mudarabah**: Profit-based savings
- **Wakalah**: Investment deposits (customer places funds with bank)
- **Murabaha**: Cost-plus financing
- **Ijara**: Leasing structures
- **Istisna'**: Forward contracting (especially real estate)
- **Sukuk**: Medium-term Islamic bonds

#### Product Categories
**Accounts:**
- Islamic Current Account (Qard Hasan)
- Islamic Savings Account (Mudarabah)
- Islamic Investment Deposit (Wakalah)
- Emirati Al Awwal Certificates (Islamic, for Emiratis)

**Financing:**
- Islamic Personal Finance
- Islamic Car Finance
- Islamic Home Finance

**Investment:**
- Sukuk investments
- Islamic credit cards

**Business Products:**
- Corporate Islamic financing
- Istisna' and Ijara for construction
- LME Murabaha
- Purchase & lease structures
- Bilateral, Club, Syndicated Islamic Finance

#### Certification
- Sharia Fatwa approved
- Supervisory Board oversight
- Multi-currency available (AED, GBP, USD, EUR)

#### Separate Operations
- Separate books for Islamic assets/liabilities/revenues/expenses
- Distinct from conventional banking operations
- Islamic-specific revenue/expense treatment

#### Pros
✓ Both conventional and Islamic options
✓ Comprehensive Islamic product suite
✓ Sharia-approved mortgages (important for home buyers)
✓ Business-focused Islamic solutions
✓ Multi-currency Islamic accounts

#### Cons
✗ Not 100% Islamic (may concern strict practitioners)

✗ Less investment product diversity than conventional arm
