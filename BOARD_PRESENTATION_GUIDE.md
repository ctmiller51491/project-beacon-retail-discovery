## 🎯 POTENTIAL OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

### **Objection 1: "Mashreq NEO already exists. Why would customers switch?"**

**Response:** "Mashreq and L'Imad have nearly identical scores on core categories (both 4.3/5 overall). But Mashreq has ZERO embedded finance offerings. That's a AED 2-3B market they've completely missed. We're launching with a full embedded ecosystem (travel, education, health, utilities). Customers will come for the convenience and stay for the ecosystem. It's the same reason Grab succeeded in SE Asia despite local competition—they build the ecosystem, not just the bank."

### **Objection 2: "Embedded finance is risky. What if partners don't want to integrate?"**

**Response:** "We're not building partnerships in isolation. Our lending team, product team, and partnerships team start this work in Wave 2A, not Wave 3. We're targeting 20+ merchants by Wave 2B and 500+ by Wave 3. For merchants, it's simple: we handle the compliance, they handle the customer experience. They get new revenue stream, we get transaction fees. It's win-win. Plus, we have sovereign backing, so we can subsidize early integrations if needed."

### **Objection 3: "UX/UI shouldn't be 37% of effort. That's too high."**

**Response:** "Actually, if we want to differentiate in the affluent segment, it has to be. Mashreq, FAB, Liv are all at 4.3-4.5★ ratings. We can't be 4.2★ and compete. Neobanks like Revolut and N26 spend 15-25% of effort on design/UX. We're at 37% because we're adding Islamic UX (prayer times, Zakat, Ramadan) that competitors don't have. That 6,010 hours in design is what gets us to 4.6★ and beats everyone else. It's not wasted—it's our competitive moat."

### **Objection 4: "Can we really launch MVP in 6 months?"**

**Response:** "Yes, because we're starting with core products only. Accounts, debit cards, investments, Islamic UX, and support. No mortgages, no lending, no credit cards in MVP. We're parallelizing across teams (backend, mobile, design, compliance all working independently). Design system is done in Month 2, infrastructure in Month 2-3, feature work Months 3-6. We have 24 people focused on this. It's aggressive but realistic. We'd rather launch a tight MVP in 6 months than a bloated launch in 12."

### **Objection 5: "How do we know AED 200-250M revenue is achievable?"**

**Response:** "We benchmarked against actual competitor data. Mashreq NEO doesn't publish revenue, but their public filings show ~AED 2B AUM with estimated AED 50-70M annual revenue. We're targeting AED 8-10B AUM—4-5x larger—but with embedded finance revenue (40-50% of total), which scales better than traditional banking. We're not guessing; we're extrapolating from comparable business models. Plus, embedded finance is proven profitable globally (Affirm, Klarna, Afterpay all profitable or heading there)."

## 🎤 FINAL TIPS

1. **Lead with the opportunity, not the product.** "AED 2-3B embedded finance market with zero competition" is more compelling than "we'll build mortgages faster than Mashreq."

2. **Use data from competitors.** Show the matrix with Mashreq at 4.3/5 and L'Imad at 4.3/5 + embedded at 4.4/5. Show why they can't copy us (they built for mass market, can't pivot to premium + embedded).

3. **Make it about Year 3.** Boards care about profitability. "We're AED 200-250M revenue, 15-20% ROAE, profitable by Year 3" is way more compelling than "we have 100K users by Year 1."

4. **Emphasize the phasing.** "MVP → Wave 2A → Wave 2B → Wave 2C → Wave 3" de-risks execution. You're not betting everything on one launch.

5. **Call out UX/UI explicitly.** Boards don't usually think about design. Saying "Design is 37% of effort—that's why we get 4.9★ rating" reframes design from "pretty" to "competitive advantage."

6. **Be ready with numbers.** When someone asks "How do you know this is achievable?", you have the answer: "We benchmarked 4 UAE banks + 8 global neobanks + analyzed 30+ Islamic fintech platforms. Here's the data..."
